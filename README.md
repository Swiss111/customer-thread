# Customer Shopping Trends Analysis (Interactive Dashboard Creation Using Power BI)

## Dataset Used - KAGGLE

Dataset: Customer Shopping Trends Dataset

* Project Preview:

The goal of this Power BI dashboard project is to analyze customer shopping behavior, payment preferences, shipping trends, demographic patterns, product category performance, promotional effectiveness, and seasonal purchasing trends to support data-driven business decisions and improve customer experience.

---

## Steps Followed

Step 1: Data was imported into Power BI from the Customer Shopping Trends dataset.

Step 2: Data cleaning and transformation were performed using Power Query Editor.

Step 3: Data validation was conducted to ensure consistency across customer demographics, payment methods, purchase amounts, product categories, and promotional data.

Step 4: Data modeling was completed to establish relationships between customers, locations, categories, payment methods, seasons, and shipping types.

Step 5: DAX measures were created to calculate Customer Count, Purchase Amount, Promo Code Usage, Payment Preferences, and Category Performance.

Step 6: KPI cards were designed to display:

* Number of Customers
* Number of Female Customers
* Number of Male Customers
* Total Payment Methods
* Total Locations

Step 7: Donut charts were created to analyze:

* Customer Distribution by Gender
* Customer Distribution by Payment Method
* Customer Distribution by Preferred Payment Method

Step 8: Bar and column charts were developed to show:

* Customer Count by Category
* Purchase Amount by Location
* Purchase Amount by Category and Promo Code Usage
* Customer Count by Item Purchased and Size

Step 9: Line charts were created to analyze:

* Customer Count by Shipping Type
* Customer Count by Season
* Customer Count by Promo Code Usage

Step 10: Stacked and clustered visualizations were developed to evaluate:

* Customer Distribution by Age Group and Gender
* Subscription Status by Payment Method
* Purchase Behavior by Product Size

Step 11: Interactive slicers were incorporated to enable dynamic filtering and detailed analysis.

## [2] Customer Demographics Analysis

### Customer Distribution by Gender

* Male customers accounted for approximately 68% of the customer base.
* Female customers accounted for approximately 32%.

Thus, male customers represent the dominant customer segment.

---

### Customer Distribution by Age Group

* Customers aged 30–60 recorded the highest customer counts.
* Age groups below 20 and above 60 contributed comparatively fewer customers.

Thus, middle-aged consumers form the largest customer segment.

---

## [3] Payment Method Analysis

### Customer Count by Payment Method

* Credit Card was the most preferred payment method with approximately 696 customers.
* Cash recorded approximately 648 customers.
* Venmo, PayPal, Debit Card, and Bank Transfer showed relatively similar usage levels.

Thus, Credit Card and Cash are the most commonly used payment methods.

---

### Payment Method and Subscription Status

* Most customers across all payment methods did not have active subscriptions.
* Credit Card users recorded the highest subscription participation.

Thus, there is significant opportunity to increase subscription adoption among customers.

---

## [4] Shipping Preferences Analysis

### Customer Count by Shipping Type

* Free Shipping generated the highest customer count at approximately 675 customers.
* Standard Shipping followed closely with approximately 654 customers.
* Two-Day Shipping recorded the lowest customer count.

Thus, customers prefer low-cost shipping options over premium delivery services.

---

## [5] Product Category Analysis

### Customer Count by Category

* Clothing recorded the highest customer count at approximately 1.7K customers.
* Accessories followed with approximately 1.2K customers.
* Footwear generated approximately 0.6K customers.
* Outerwear recorded the lowest customer count.

Thus, Clothing is the most popular shopping category among customers.

---

### Purchase Amount by Category and Promo Code Usage

* Clothing generated the highest purchase amount.
* Accessories ranked second in total spending.
* Promo code usage positively influenced spending across major categories.

Thus, promotional campaigns are most effective within Clothing and Accessories categories.

---

## [6] Location Performance Analysis

### Purchase Amount by Location

* California generated the highest purchase amount of approximately 5.6K USD.
* Alabama, Arkansas, Alaska, and Delaware also recorded strong purchase values.
* Florida generated comparatively lower purchase amounts among the displayed states.

Thus, California represents the strongest revenue-generating location.

---

### Purchase Amount by Location and Promo Code Usage

* Locations with higher promo code participation generally generated stronger purchase amounts.
* California benefited significantly from promotional activity.

Thus, location-based promotional campaigns can help increase customer spending.

---

## [7] Seasonal Shopping Analysis

### Customer Count by Season

* Spring recorded the highest customer count at approximately 999 customers.
* Fall and Winter showed similar customer activity levels.
* Summer recorded the lowest customer count at approximately 955 customers.

Thus, Spring is the strongest season for customer engagement and sales opportunities.

---

## [8] Promotional Analysis

### Customer Count by Promo Code Usage

* Customers not using promo codes totaled 2,223.
* Customers using promo codes totaled 1,677.

Thus, the majority of customers make purchases without relying on promotional discounts.

---

## [9] Product Purchase Analysis

### Customer Count by Item Purchased

* Blouse recorded the highest customer purchase count.
* Jewelry, Pants, Shirts, Dresses, and Sweaters also showed strong customer demand.

Thus, fashion-related products dominate customer purchasing behavior.

---

### Customer Count by Item Purchased and Size

* Medium (M) and Small (S) sizes generated the highest purchase volumes.
* Large (L), Extra Large (XL), and other sizes recorded comparatively lower demand.

Thus, Medium and Small sizes represent the most preferred product sizes among customers.

---

## Recommendations

1. Increase marketing efforts toward male customers, who represent the majority of the customer base.
2. Expand inventory within the Clothing and Accessories categories due to their strong customer demand.
3. Prioritize stock availability for Medium and Small product sizes to reduce stock-out risks.
4. Introduce targeted promotional campaigns to increase promo code adoption and customer engagement.
5. Focus marketing investments in high-performing locations such as California, Alabama, and Arkansas.
6. Leverage Spring seasonal demand by launching promotional campaigns ahead of peak shopping periods.
7. Encourage subscription enrollment through exclusive offers and loyalty rewards.
8. Continue supporting Credit Card and Cash payment options while promoting digital payment adoption.
9. Improve customer retention strategies for high-demand product categories such as Clothing, Jewelry, and Accessories.
10. Optimize shipping strategies by maintaining attractive Free Shipping and Standard Shipping options that align with customer preferences.
